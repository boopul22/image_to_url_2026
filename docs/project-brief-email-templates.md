# Project Brief — Email Reply Templates

Templates to reply to leads submitted via `https://imagetourl.cloud/project-brief`.
Copy-paste, then fill the `{{placeholders}}`.

---

## 1. First reply — ask for full requirements + references

**Subject:** Re: your website project — a few questions before I quote

```
Hi {{first_name}},

Thanks for sending the brief — got it loud and clear.

Before I put a number on this, I want to make sure I'm quoting the RIGHT
thing, not my guess of it. Could you reply to this email with:

  1. A longer version of what you want built, in your own words.
     Don't worry about format — bullet list, voice note transcript,
     screenshot, rough sketch, whatever's fastest for you. The more
     detail, the tighter I can make the quote.

  2. 1–3 reference websites you'd like yours to look or work like.
     Paste the URLs and note which part you like (the hero, the
     pricing page, how fast it loads, the checkout flow, etc).
     Even "I hate all of these and want the opposite" is useful.

  3. Anything you want me to AVOID — frameworks, platforms, past
     agencies, design clichés, things that burned you last time.

If it's easier to re-fill the brief form with the extra detail,
here's the link again:
  → https://imagetourl.cloud/project-brief

Once I have that, I'll send back a fixed-price quote, timeline,
and what's in / out of scope — usually within 24 hours.

Thanks,
Bipul
imagetourl.cloud
```

## Tone guide

- First reply within **24 hours** or the lead cools fast.
- Write like a human, not a proposal doc. One idea per line.
- Always repeat their reference URLs back — shows you actually read it.
- Always include the `https://imagetourl.cloud/project-brief` link
  so they can extend the brief without digging through old email.
- Never say "looking forward to hearing from you" — dead phrase,
  end with what *you* will do next instead.
