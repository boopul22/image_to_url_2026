# Product

## Register

product

## Users

People who need a shareable image URL *right now*: forum and Reddit posters, marketplace sellers (eBay, Etsy), no-code builders (Zapier/Make/n8n), developers testing an API or README, and non-technical users moving a photo from phone to web. Most arrive from search, on a task, often on mobile. Guests are the majority and are first-class; signing in (Google) adds permanence and a larger, size-priced allowance.

## Product Purpose

imagetourl.cloud is the fastest path from an image file to a hosted, CDN-backed URL. Free tier funded by ads. Success = time-to-copied-link measured in seconds, and users who come back because the limits, costs, and expiry were exactly what the site said they were.

## Brand Personality

Fast, honest, effortless. The tool disappears into the task: no signup walls, no countdown timers, no surprises. Numbers are real (100 uploads/day guests, 150 credits/day signed-in, ~2-week guest expiry) and always visible before the user commits.

## Anti-references

- Dark-pattern file hosts: fake download buttons, countdown timers, limits buried in fine print, "premium" nag walls.
- Ad-farm image hosts where the tool drowns in ad units and the upload button is the hardest thing to find.
- Generic SaaS-cream template landing kits — gradient heroes, glassmorphism cards, hero-metric blocks.

## Design Principles

1. **The URL is the product.** Every screen optimizes time-to-copied-link; anything that delays or distracts from the link must earn its place.
2. **State honesty.** Quotas, credit costs, and expiry are visible *before* the user commits, not revealed as errors after.
3. **One visual vocabulary.** The soft square-edged system (square corners, recessed inner shadows, hairline borders, rose accent) everywhere; the rounded `.soft-uploader` is the single deliberate exception.
4. **Guests are first-class.** Inform, never nag: expiry notes and sign-in prompts are helpful context at the moment they matter, never gates.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA: ≥4.5:1 body-text contrast, keyboard-operable uploader (drop zone is a focusable button), visible `:focus-visible` indicators, `prefers-reduced-motion` respected, touch targets ≥44px on interactive controls. Light-mode only by design. 28 locales incl. RTL (ar, fa, ur) — copy changes must consider localized surfaces.
