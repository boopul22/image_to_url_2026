🔬 Research the copy is grounded in

Before writing, I pulled current signal on what people actually want from image-to-URL / hosting tools (last30days skill + grounded web). The copy uses their language, not invented marketing.

The Imgur exodus is live
Imgur removed anonymous uploads (2023), pushes its app, ran an NSFW purge, and geo-blocked the UK (Sep 2025). People are actively searching for a replacement - so the pages lean on no-signup / anonymous and contrast with Imgur where natural.
"A direct link that embeds"
The repeated ask across Reddit/Discord roundups: a URL that renders as the image in Discord, Reddit and forums (not an attachment or gallery), permanent, no compression. That phrasing is now used verbatim across the pages - and it's exactly the "link" intent.

All copy then went through a humanizer pass: no em-dashes, no rule-of-three filler, no "seamlessly / effortlessly / unlock", varied sentence length. Product claims kept accurate (free, no signup, no watermark, up to 10MB, no "stored forever").
